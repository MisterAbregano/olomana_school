📖 Student Handbook

Mr. R Abregano

Mr. R Abregano is a Native Hawaiian cultural educator and practitioner rooted in the traditional practices of hele lālau (subsistence hunting), lawaiʻa (traditional fishing), mele (Hawaiian chant and song), and hula protocol (ceremonial practices of dance and ritual).

Raised in the values of aloha ʻāina (love for the land) and kuleana (responsibility), he brings ʻike kupuna (ancestral knowledge) into modern educational spaces with intention and heart.

He is passionate about learning from and with his haumāna (students), seeking to understand their lived experiences so he can offer grounded, alternative perspectives. Through his cultural upbringing, he has been encouraged to cultivate substance within our keiki (children)—not only in what they learn, but in who they become.

His approach honors identity, purpose, and the deep connections between people and place.
