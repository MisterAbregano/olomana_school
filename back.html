I ka wā tatau

Born and raised in Honolulu, Hawaiʻi, Mr. R Abregano — affectionately known as “Mr. A” — is a lifelong learner, sports fan, and committed Hawaiian cultural practitioner. Though he once found his greatest peace in the ocean, a gradual loss of vision shifted his course. In response, he embraced new passions: Brazilian Jiu-Jitsu, video games, and a deeper connection to his Hawaiian cultural foundations.

Mr. A dances hula alongside his awesome and talented wife, Mrs. Alexis — also known lovingly as Mrs. A 🌺. They both are in preparations for an exciting hula competition in Hilo, Hawaiʻi. His favorite pastime is singing and playing traditional Hawaiian music.

He is a lifelong fan of Detroit, Michigan — 313 "Motor City" — a place known for its blue-collar GRIT and the pursuit of the American dream: fast cars, Motown music, Megatron, and good ol’ Barry. When he says “Detroit vs. The World,” it’s about resilience: backing your home no matter the odds. From the Lions to the Pistons, Red Wings, and Tigers, Mr. A supports them all.

At Olomana School, Mr. A brings that same GRIT, creativity, and kuleana to uplift haumāna through culture, humor, and high expectations.

“Hopefully, I will have the opportunity to build on my own dreams. I look forward to meeting each of you and offering encouragement throughout your time at Olomana. My wife and I are currently preparing for an exciting hula competition in Hilo, Hawaiʻi.”

E aloha pū,
Mr. R Abregano
